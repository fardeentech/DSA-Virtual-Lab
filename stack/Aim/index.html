Stack

Learning Objectives of the Experiment

In this experiment, we will be able to do the following:

  • Understand the basic operations of a Stack, including push, pop, and peek.
  • Implement a Stack using arrays or linked lists and explore different methods of representing a Stack.
  • Apply Stack concepts to solve problems like reversing a string, checking balanced parentheses, and evaluating postfix expressions.
  • Analyze the time complexity of Stack operations and understand the trade-offs involved in different implementations.
  • Compare Stack-based algorithms with other data structures and recognize the advantages of LIFO (Last In, First Out) behavior in specific scenarios.
